# Content Strategy — Lisa Fit Method
_Upgraded May 2026 — incorporating external review + 2026 platform data (OpusClip 13.5M clip analysis)_

---

## What Changed From the Previous Version

The previous strategy was too educational. Excellent for authority and conversion, weak for **follower growth**. Educational content builds the audience you already have. Identity and personality content is what **attracts the audience you don't have yet.**

Data from a 13.5M viral clip analysis (OpusClip, Jan–Mar 2026) confirms the external reviewer's instinct:
- The #1 performing hook type: **Product/Outcome Showcase** — show the result in the first 2 seconds, then explain
- 77.8% of viral clips use B-roll or demonstration rather than talking-head openers
- The "warm expert" tone (calm, credible, substantive) accounts for 25.7% of all viral fitness content — this is exactly your natural voice
- Multi-part progression series generate 30%+ higher engagement than single videos and are the fastest organic follow-driver on TikTok

The adjustments are **not** about becoming a different creator. They're about adding the identity layer that makes people follow you, not just watch you once.

## The 60/40 Rule

| Type | % of Content | Function |
|---|---|---|
| Educational | 60% | Authority, saves, conversion |
| Identity/Personality/Relatability | 40% | Follower growth, shares, community |

Previously: ~80% educational, ~20% personality. This is excellent for building trust with people already in your funnel. It does not grow your top of funnel.

**The sequence is:** Identity content → follower → educational content → trust → conversion.
You can't skip step 1.

## Your Brand Voice — Own It Completely

You are not:
- Hyper-sexualized
- Loud or chaotic
- Motivational-poster energy
- A "wellness girl" with green smoothies

You are:
- **Intelligent, dry, calm** — you sound like someone who knows things without needing to announce it
- **Non-performative** — you don't perform confidence, you have it
- **Introvert-confident** — you're not shy, you're just not loud
- **Engineer brain in a fitness context** — this is genuinely rare and should run through everything

This IS your differentiation. The fitness space is full of loud energy. Your quiet credibility is the contrast. **Don't sand it down to sound more "influencer." It's the reason people will follow you specifically.**

## The 9 Content Series

Run one series at a time — 5–7 days, then move on. Series create:
- Return viewers (algorithm rewards watch time from same viewer)
- Comments ("can't wait for Part 3")
- Saves ("bookmarked to find later")
- Binge behavior (TikTok pushes your other videos when someone finishes one)

**Visual identity rule:** Every video in the same series should look similar — same location, same on-screen text style, same hook delivery. So viewers instantly recognize it's you.

### Series 1 — WISH I KNEW *(identity + authority — double down)*

The most powerful series you have. Combines: vulnerability, authority, relatability, story. People share these because they've had the same experience.

**Critical rule from 2026 data:** Open with the **cost** of not knowing, not the setup. "I wasted 2 years because nobody told me this" beats "Let me tell you something I wish I knew."

| Episode | Hook |
|---|---|
| 1 | "I trained consistently for 2 years and my body barely changed. This is why." |
| 2 | "I thought soreness meant I had a good workout. I was completely wrong." |
| 3 | "I thought lifting would make me bulky. Here's what actually happened." |
| 4 | "I thought cardio was enough. I had no idea how wrong I was." |
| 5 | "I wish someone had explained progressive overload to me before I wasted years." |
| 6 | "I didn't know the hip hinge existed before I hurt my back. I do now." |
| 7 | "I spent money on supplements before I understood my training. Never again." |
| 8 | "I used to judge whether a workout was good by how tired I was. That was the problem." |

**Why these work:** Every one of these has its own TikTok discover page. The "I wish I knew" format is actively trending. The combination of vulnerability + lesson + authority is the "warm expert" tone that performs best.

### Series 2 — DO THIS, NOT THAT *(education + demonstration — high save rate)*

Form comparison. The "not that" is always relatable — it's what most beginners do. The contrast creates the "oh, that's me" moment that drives saves.

**Format:** B-roll of both versions. Voiceover or text explaining why. 30–45 seconds.

| Episode | Movement |
|---|---|
| 1 | Squat: common mistake vs. the fix |
| 2 | Hip hinge: how it goes wrong and why it matters |
| 3 | Row: most people grip and pull — this is what to do instead |
| 4 | Push-up: the shoulder blade problem nobody talks about |
| 5 | Romanian deadlift: the thing everyone does that defeats the purpose |
| 6 | Overhead press: why your lower back hurts (and how to stop it) |
| 7 | Plank: you're probably not bracing correctly |

**The comment hook:** End each one with "What's the movement you've been doing wrong for years? Drop it below." — generates high comment volume because everyone has one.

### Series 3 — PULL-UP PROGRESSION *(multi-part — the follow-driver)*

Multi-part content is the fastest way to convert viewers to followers. The hook is the anticipation: "follow so you don't miss Part 3."

**Rule:** Film all parts before posting Part 1. Post every 24–48 hours. Include "Part X/4" visibly on-screen from the first second.

| Part | Hook | Content |
|---|---|---|
| Part 1 | "You can't do a pull-up because you're skipping this." | Why grip strength and lat activation are the real prerequisites — not arm strength |
| Part 2 | "The 3 movements that build pull-up strength faster than doing assisted pull-ups." | Band pull-aparts, lat pulldown with pause, dead hang progression |
| Part 3 | "4 weeks in — where I actually am." | Honest progress check: what changed, what didn't, what adjusted |
| Part 4 | "First unassisted pull-up." | The moment. Show the actual rep. Don't narrate over it — let it land. |

### Series 5 — HARSH TRUTHS *(short opinion takes — authority building)*

Strong positioning. Not rage-bait. The "experienced honesty" tone, not "lecturer" tone. Keep these under 40 seconds. One truth per video.

**The key:** State the truth in the first 3 seconds. Don't set it up. Don't give context first. Lead with the claim.

| Episode | The Take |
|---|---|
| 1 | "Most people don't need more workouts. They need consistency with the ones they have." |
| 2 | "If your program changes every week, that's not variety. That's the problem." |
| 3 | "Classes are not the same as progressive strength training. They can coexist — but don't confuse them." |
| 4 | "Most women are under-eating protein by a significant margin. Not by a little." |
| 5 | "You probably don't need supplements. You probably need to sleep more." |
| 6 | "Cardio doesn't build muscle. Strength training does. The body you want comes from the second one." |
| 7 | "Soreness is not a measure of workout quality. It's a measure of novelty." |
| 8 | "If you're too sore to train, you went too hard. That's not a badge. That's a miscalibration." |

**The comment play:** End with "What's your controversial fitness opinion?" — generates both agreement and pushback, both drive distribution.

## Community Bait: How to Actually Drive Comments

Comments are more valuable than likes for reach. Comments tell the algorithm this content is starting conversations.

**Tactics that work:**

**1. Ask a specific question — not a generic one**
BAD: "What do you think?" → everyone ignores
GOOD: "What's the movement that took you the longest to feel? For me it was the hip hinge — 6 months before I felt it right."

You go first. Model the answer. Make it easy to respond.

**2. Binary choices drive volume**
"Team morning workout or team evening workout — you can't say both."
"Gym shoes: sneakers or lifting shoes? Lifters changed my squat."
"Do you use straps for deadlift or no straps? Hot take incoming."

**3. Vulnerability first creates reciprocal vulnerability**
"My most embarrassing gym moment: I loaded a bar wrong and had to ask someone to help unload it in front of my whole gym. What's yours?"

**4. "Am I the only one" is gold**
"Am I the only one who has to psych themselves up for deadlift day even after 5 years of doing them?"

These generate "no, me too" responses — which are comments — which drive reach.

**5. The debate (non-rage-bait version)**
"Controversial: steady-state cardio at the gym is mostly a waste of time for women who want to build muscle. Here's what the actual mechanism is."
State your position, explain the mechanism, let the disagreement come. You don't need to respond to every comment — the volume itself does the work.

## What's Missing: More Face-to-Camera

The data says 77.8% of viral clips open with B-roll or demonstration. BUT — your personality is the product. Face-to-camera is where people fall in love with a creator, not just a channel.

**The balance:**
- Educational form videos: B-roll or demonstration first, then explain
- Identity content: face-to-camera from the first second
- Short takes and opinions: face-to-camera only
- "Wish I knew": face-to-camera (the emotion needs to land)

You are articulate, calm, and sound intelligent without performing it. That is rare. More face-to-camera, not less — especially for the Gym Girl Reality and Harsh Truths series.

## Progression Series: The Follow Driver

Multi-part series are the fastest organic follow-driver on TikTok/Reels. The algorithm rewards them (watch time chains), viewers follow to see what happens, and comment sections become community hubs.

**The rules:**
1. Film all parts before posting Part 1. Never post a Part 1 without the rest ready.
2. Post Part 1 to 3 within 3–4 days total. Do not wait a week between parts.
3. Put "Part X/Y" visibly on-screen from the first frame. Never make viewers guess where they are.
4. End Part 1 with a specific cliffhanger — not "stay tuned" but "In Part 2 I'll show you the exact cue that fixed this for me."
5. Pin Part 1 to your profile while the series is running.

**Progression series to run one at a time:**

| Month | Series | Parts |
|---|---|---|
| Month 1 | Pull-Up Progression | 4 parts (see Series 3 above) |
| Month 2 | Push-Up to Full | 3 parts |
| Month 3 | Hip Thrust Progression | 3 parts |
| Month 4 | Deadlift From Scratch | 4 parts |

## Filming Guide: How to Batch 2 Hours Into a Week of Content

### The 2-Hour Session Breakdown

**30 min — Setup and prep**
- Review your list for the day (have this written before you arrive)
- Set up tripod/phone angle for the first shot type
- Change into filming outfit if different from workout clothes
- No perfectionism here — just set up and start

**60 min — Film**
- Target: 5–8 clips. Each clip = 2–3 takes, pick the best one
- Start with whatever you're most confident about
- Talking-head clips: film in one uninterrupted take if possible. Authenticity > polish
- Form/demonstration clips: film from two angles (side and front). Pick later
- If something isn't working after 3 takes, skip it and come back

**30 min — Sort and label**
- Delete clearly bad takes in camera roll immediately
- Label by category: `gymgirl_introvert.mov`, `dosthis_squat.mov`, `progression_pullup_p1.mov`
- You're not editing now — you're organizing so editing is fast later

### Editing: The Non-Negotiables

1. **Burned-in captions** — 80.2% of viral content has them. Silent viewers are a huge percentage of your reach. Use CapCut's auto-caption and clean up mistakes. This is not optional.
2. **First 2 seconds must show something** — the lift, the face, the text hook. Not a logo. Not an intro.
3. **Cut everything before the point** — if the first 3 seconds aren't the hook, delete them
4. **Optimal length: 15–45 seconds for identity/opinion, 45–75 seconds for educational**
5. **No music required for talking-head content** — your voice IS the audio

## Platform-Specific Notes

### TikTok — Growth Platform
- Post 1–2x/day
- Trending audio on 30% of videos (not all — authenticity wins over trend-chasing)
- Post at peak hours: 6–10am or 7–10pm in your timezone
- Use ~10 hashtags: mix niche (#girlswholift, #strengthtrainingwomen, #beginnerfitness) with broad (#fyp, #fitness, #workouttips). Videos with #fyp get 2.2× median views.
- Engage with comments within the first 60 minutes of posting — this is when the algorithm tests reach

### Instagram — Conversion Platform
- 1 Reel/day (same video as TikTok, remove watermark via CapCut)
- 3 carousels/week (education content — highest save rate on Instagram)
- 3–5 Stories/day (tip, poll, progress, program reminder, real life)
- Carousels: 6–10 slides, minimal text per slide, end with a CTA or question
- Reels: shorter than TikTok versions — Instagram rewards 15–30 seconds more than 60+ for reach

### Reddit (r/xxfitness) — Credibility Platform
- 1–2 posts per week
- **Never pitch, never link to the program in the post itself**
- Education posts only — the 5 movement patterns, progressive overload explained, injury prevention topics
- Answer questions in comments daily (20 minutes) — your engineering + CPT combination stands out
- Add your website link to your Reddit bio only
- After 6 weeks of consistent valuable comments, you have a post history that functions as a credential

## Voice Check — For Every Piece Before Posting

Three questions. All three must be yes.

1. **Would a typical influencer say this?** → If yes, revise. Your differentiation is that you don't sound like them.
2. **Does it explain WHY, not just WHAT?** → Educational content needs a mechanism. Identity content needs honesty. Neither should be vague.
3. **Does it start with something worth watching?** → Play the first 2 seconds. Is there already a reason to keep watching? If not, cut everything before that moment.
