#### 1. Meg Squats — Stronger by the Day

**Program:** Stronger by the Day app + Before the Barbell (free 8-week beginner intro)
**Price:** $12/month (free 7-day trial) — ~$144/year
**Members:** 22,000+ active users
**Payment model:** Monthly subscription

**Transformation promise (verbatim):**
> "The app you've been missing to ACTUALLY get stronger!"
> "Simply answers questions before you even have them."

**Target audience language:** Women serious about building strength; beginners intimidated by "how heavy should I go?"; people who want "exercise demos that show exactly what to do without having to guess."

**Program structure:**
- Gym, Express, or Bodyweight options (switch anytime)
- 3, 4, or 5-day frequency options
- Video library with written instructions
- "Before the Barbell" = free entry-level 8-week program with zero barrier to test the brand

**What she does well:**
- Free intro program builds massive trust before the paid ask
- App flexibility (gym vs. home, frequency changes) removes objections
- Barbell-focused, which attracts serious beginners
- Warm, approachable creator persona

**What she doesn't do:**
- No structured curriculum on *why* — the app tells you *what*, not the system behind it
- Subscription model means ongoing cost forever
- No injury credibility or rebuild story
- Engineering/systems-thinking angle is absent

**Top buyer complaints (from reviews):**
1. Subscription cost feels hard to justify once you've learned the movements
2. App is flexible but can feel unstructured for complete beginners who want a clear 4–12 week arc
3. No explicit movement quality education — you get workouts, not a foundation course

**Lisa's edge over Meg Squats:**
- One-time $47 vs. $144/year — same result, no ongoing cost
- Lisa teaches the system (WHY) not just the workout (WHAT)
- Lisa's injury/rebuild story adds a layer of credibility Meg doesn't have
- Lisa's program is explicitly designed to be a foundation — Meg's is a long-term app subscription

2. Coaching is too expensive for most entry-level buyers
3. Lacks a single clear "start here" path — the brand is comprehensive but complex to navigate

**Lisa's edge over GGS:**
- $47 one-time vs. $299/month — Lisa is 72× cheaper for coaching-equivalent outcomes for beginners

#### 4. Caroline Girvan — EPIC Series (YouTube)

**Programs:** EPIC I, EPIC II, EPIC III, IRON Series, Fuel Series, GROW Series, Beginner EPIC
**Price:** Free (YouTube)
**Payment model:** No charge — monetized through YouTube ad revenue

**Program overview:**
- 10-week EPIC program: 50 workouts, 45 seconds on / 15 seconds off format

- Follows along with you for the full workout duration
- No narration during sets — timer-based, music-driven

**What she does well:**
- Completely free eliminates every price objection
- Incredibly structured (week-by-week schedule in a PDF)
- Backed by sound science with visible progressive overload
- Massive community — creates accountability through YouTube comments and Facebook groups
- Produces visible, documented results for committed users

**What she doesn't do:**
- Teaches ZERO of the "why" — no coaching cues, no movement education
- Programs are notoriously difficult — coaches suggest IRON Series for beginners since EPIC I is too hard
- No workout tracking (no built-in progress system)
- No injury awareness or corrective focus — users have reported back and shoulder injuries
- One-size-fits-all — no weight recommendations, no form corrections

**Top buyer complaints (from reviews):**
1. "Notoriously difficult" — intimidating for true beginners, requires the separate Beginner EPIC as a prerequisite
2. No exercise coaching cues — users have no idea if they're doing movements correctly
3. No tracking system — you have to build your own logging
4. Progress plateau after completing the series — no clear "what next"

**Lisa's edge over Caroline Girvan:**
- Lisa teaches movement quality; Caroline assumes it
- Lisa's program has built-in tracking; Caroline's doesn't
- Lisa's 3 days/week is manageable; Caroline's daily workouts create burnout risk
- Lisa's foundation focus is exactly what Caroline's beginners need before attempting EPIC
- Lisa explains the WHY behind every movement; Caroline just demonstrates

**Strategic insight:** Caroline Girvan's audience is Lisa's ideal buyer. People who started EPIC, got hurt or lost, or felt like they "couldn't keep up" need exactly what Lisa offers. Lisa's positioning as "the foundation program before programs like EPIC" is a direct market capture move.

**Framework:** Template D (Comeback Arc)

The "four changes of workout clothes" level of specificity is what makes this hit — Lisa should open her version with a similarly specific, sensory detail.

## Pain Point Language Master List

The following verbatim phrases come from real women in the research above. Use these exact words in content — they are proven to resonate because they are the actual language your audience uses:

**About failure/plateau:**
